About

What is AYUSH?

India's Ancient Healing Heritage

AYUSH is an acronym for India's six traditional health systems — Ayurveda, Yoga & Naturopathy, Unani, Siddha, and Homeopathy. These systems have been practised for thousands of years and form the backbone of healthcare for millions.

The Ministry of AYUSH was established in 2014 to promote, develop, and propagate these indigenous and alternative medicine systems across India and the world.

This Virtual Herbal Garden bridges the gap between ancient botanical knowledge and modern digital access — letting anyone explore, learn, and understand medicinal plants from home.

Siddha Tradition
The 18 Siddhars of Tamil Nadu develop one of the world's oldest medical systems, using herbs, minerals, and spiritual practices.

Unani Arrives in India
Greek medicine travels via Persia and the Arab world, blending with Indian traditions during the Mughal period to form Unani.

200 BCE

Patanjali's Yoga Sutras systematize yoga practice. Naturopathy roots grow from nature-cure traditions across cultures.

1796 CE
Homeopathy Founded
Samuel Hahnemann founds homeopathy in Germany. It reaches India in 1839 and grows to become the world's largest homeopathy practice.
